Module 6: Final Presentation

Overview

Every insight from the preceding five modules synthesized into a single cohesive 17-section presentation built in PowerPoint and exported to PDF. The deck is structured as an executive report moving from summary through methodology, results, dashboard, discussion, and conclusion.

Tools: PowerPoint, PDF export

What I Did

  • Designed a 17-section presentation with a consistent dark navy and teal color scheme, numbered section cards, and subtitle captions that orient the audience before each slide’s content
  • Opened with an Executive Summary presenting four quadrant panels covering Programming Languages, Database Technologies, Job Market Demand, and Developer Demographics — each with three evidence-backed bullets
  • Built an Introduction slide leading with four prominent stat cards: 65,000+ survey respondents, 180+ countries represented, 27,005 job records analyzed, and 3 dashboard tabs built
  • Presented Programming Language Trends as side-by-side horizontal bar charts comparing current usage against desired next year for the top 10 languages
  • Repeated the Results then Findings and Implications structure for Database Trends, then produced a Job Postings slide with a horizontal bar chart of 12 technologies ordered by descending count
  • Devoted three slides to the dashboard with full-screenshot slides per tab with annotation
  • Synthesized a Discussion slide across five cross-cutting themes: Technology Convergence, Job Market Alignment, Cloud Platform Dominance, Demographic Concentration, and the rise of NoSQL and in-memory databases
  • Exported the completed deck to PDF and submitted for AI grading and peer review

Key Findings

Python’s job market demand is growing faster than current postings reflect — C dominates at 25,114 postings while Python sits fourth at 1,173, yet tops the desired languages chart. The same four technologies surface across every data source: Python, JavaScript/TypeScript, PostgreSQL, and AWS appear at or near the top of every relevant chart. When multiple independent data sources agree on the same signal, it is worth trusting.

Conclusion

Technology Landscape is Evolving Rapidly — Python and TypeScript are displacing older paradigms. PostgreSQL consolidates as the relational database of choice. AWS leads cloud adoption across all developer segments.

Job Market Reflects Survey Trends — C, Java, and JavaScript dominate raw hiring volume. Python signals an outsized and growing data-focused presence.

Demographics Inform Talent Strategy — 68.6% of developers are aged 25–44. US and India represent the largest respondent bases. Bachelor’s degree is the dominant credential.

Data-Driven Insights Enable Real-World Decisions — A rigorous pipeline from collection through wrangling, visualization, dashboard, and presentation transforms survey data into actionable intelligence.
